There exists some integer $n\ge 0$ such that each point of $X$ has a neighborhood
homeomorphic to an open subset of the closed upper half-space $\mathbb R^n_+$, where

$\quad\quad\mathbb R^n_+:=\{(x_1,\dots,x_n)\in\mathbb R^n : x_n\ge 0\}$

has the Euclidean subspace topology.
Equivalently, each point has a neighborhood homeomorphic to $\mathbb R^n_+$.
Note that the value of $n$ is not allowed to differ between points;
if it can vary, then the space has the weaker property {P235}.

In the case $n=0$, the half-space $\mathbb R^0_+$ is considered the same as $\mathbb R^0=\{0\}$;
thus a point having a neighborhood homeomorphic to
$\mathbb R^0_+$ means it is an isolated point.

Equivalently when $n>0$, each point $p\in X$ satisfies one of the following:
- (*"manifold interior" point*) $p$ has a neighborhood homeomorphic to $\mathbb R^n;$
- (*"manifold boundary" point*) $p$ has a neighborhood homeomorphic to $\mathbb R^n_+$
with $p$ mapped to a point of $\partial\mathbb R^n_+:=\{x\in\mathbb R^n: x_n=0\}$.

*Note*: In all the above, one can equivalently require the neighborhoods to be open.

This property is the main ingredient in the definition of {P237}.
