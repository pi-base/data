Every point in $X$ has a countable local $\pi$-base.

A *local $\pi$-base* for $x \in X$ is a collection $\mathcal{V}_x$ of nonempty open sets in $X$ such that every neighbourhood $U$ of $x$ contains some $V \in \mathcal V_x$.
(Note: We don't require that $x \in V$, i.e., the elements of $\mathcal{V}_x$ need not be neighbourhoods of $x$.)

Such a space has countable $\pi$-character, where the *$\pi$-character* is defined as

$\quad\quad\pi\chi(X):=\sup\{\pi\chi(x,X):x\in X\}+\omega$

with $\pi\chi(x,X)$ being the minimum size of a local $\pi$-base for $x$.

Defined on page 16 of {{zb:0559.54003}}.

----
#### Meta-properties

- This property is hereditary with respect to dense sets.
- $X$ satisfies this property iff its Kolmogorov quotient $\operatorname{Kol}(X)$ does.
- This property is preserved by countable products.
