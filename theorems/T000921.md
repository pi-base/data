Let $A$ be a closed discrete subset of $X$.
Since {T2}, $X$ is {P21}, which implies that $A$ is finite.
By inductively applying the {P3} property, one can cover $A$ by pairwise disjoint open sets,
each meeting $A$ in exactly one point.

(Note that due to {T19}, the assumption of {P19} is equivalent here to {P21}.)
