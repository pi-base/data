If $X$ is finite, then it's automatically discrete ([Explore](https://topology.pi-base.org/spaces?q=Finite+%2B+Ordinal+space+%2B+%7EDiscrete)). Thus take $X\geq \omega$ to be infinite, then consider the subset $S=\{\beta + 1 : \beta +1 < X\}$ of successor ordinals lesser than $X$. Every point in $S$ is isolated, hence $S$ is discrete, hence by the fact that $|S|=|X|$ and Toronto, $X$ is discrete as well.
