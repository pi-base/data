Suppose we have a closed set $A$ and a point $x\notin A$.
Let $(U_n)_{n\in \mathbb{N}}$ be a countable neighbourhood basis for $x$.
Since $X$ is {P3}, the (countably many) open sets $X\setminus\overline{U_n}$ cover $A$.
Since $A$ is closed in $X$, it is also {P19};
consequently $A\subseteq\bigcup_{n\in I}(X\setminus\overline{U_n})$ for some finite $I\subseteq\mathbb N$.
Taking complements, one sees that $\bigcap_{n\in I}\overline{U_n}$, which is a closed neighborhood of $x$, is disjoint from $A$.
