Pick a point $x$ and a closed set $A$ with $x\notin A$. By {P28}, let $(U_n)_{n\in \mathbb{N}}$ be a countable neighbourhood basis for $x$.
The sets $A\setminus \overline{U_n}$ are an open cover of $A$ from {P3}, and so by {P19} there exists a finite subcover $\mathcal{V}$.
Then $U := \bigcap\{U_n \mid X \setminus \overline{U_n} \in \mathcal{V}\}$ is a neighborhood of $x$ with $\overline{U}\cap A = \emptyset$.
