If $X$ is non-trivial, choose distinct $x,y \in X$, and
by Complete Hausdorff there is a continuous
\(f:X \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) with \(f(x)=0 \neq 1=f(y)\).

Asserted on page 223 of {{doi:10.1007/978-1-4612-6290-9}}.
