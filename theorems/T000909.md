Let $(V_n(x))_{n\in\mathbb N}$ be the weak-base sequence at $x$ from
{P228}. First, for each $x\in X$ there is an index $n(x)$ such that
$V_{n(x)}(x)\subseteq U$ for every open neighborhood $U$ of $x$. Otherwise,
for each $n$ choose an open neighborhood $U_n$ of $x$ such that
$V_n(x)\nsubseteq U_n$. Since $X$ is a {P147}, the set
$W=\bigcap_{n\in\mathbb N}U_n$ is open and contains $x$. By {P228}, some
$V_m(x)\subseteq W\subseteq U_m$, a contradiction.

Let $N(x)=\bigcap\{U:U\text{ is an open neighborhood of }x\}$ be the
neighborhood kernel of $x$. The first paragraph gives
$V_{n(x)}(x)\subseteq N(x)$. If $y\in N(x)$, then every open neighborhood
of $x$ is an open neighborhood of $y$. Applying the first paragraph at
$y$, there is an index $n(y)$ such that $V_{n(y)}(y)$ is contained in
every open neighborhood of $x$, hence $V_{n(y)}(y)\subseteq N(x)$.

By the openness criterion in {P228}, each $N(x)$ is open. Thus every point
has an open neighborhood kernel, so $X$ is {P90}.
