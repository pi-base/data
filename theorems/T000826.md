For any $x \in X$, the collection of open neighborhoods of $x$ must have a minimal element $U$. Since for any other open neighborhood $V$, $V \cap U$ is an open neighborhood as well, we must have $U \subseteq V \cap U \subseteq U$, thus $U = V \cap U$, implying uniqueness of $U$.
