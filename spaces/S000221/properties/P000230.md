  name: Answer to "Are minimal neighborhoods in an Alexandrov topology path-connected?"

A successor ordinal $\alpha=\beta+1\in X$ has a smallest neighborhood,
namely $[\alpha,\to)=(\beta,\to)$, which is open and {P199} (see {{mathse:2965374}}).
Similarly, $\alpha=0$ has $X$ has unique neighborhood, which is open and {P199}.
And if $\alpha\in X$ is a limit ordinal, every neighborhood of $\alpha$ contains
an open neighborhood of the form $(\gamma,\to)=[\gamma+1,\to)$ for some $\gamma<\alpha$.
This last neighborhood is thus also {P199}.

By {T583}, all these neighborhoods are {P200}.
