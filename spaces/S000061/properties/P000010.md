  name: Counterexamples in Topology

For any nonempty set $U\subset \mathbb R\setminus\mathbb Q$ open in $X$ there exist numbers $a<b$ such that $(a,b)\setminus\mathbb Q \subset U$.
Then $[a,b]\subset \overline{U}$ and $\mathrm{int}(\overline{U})\supset (a,b)$ is not contained in $U$.
