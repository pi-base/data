For all $a,b,c,d \in X$, note that due to the Chinese remainder theorem, $U_a(b)$ and $U_c(d)$ are either disjoint or meet in infinitely many points.
But if $X$ had a cut point $p$, we would find $a',b',c',d'\in X$ with $U_{a'}(b')\cap U_{c'}(d') = \{p\}$. Contradiction.
