Let $x \in X$.

If $x \in C$, then the sets $\{x\}\cup (x- \delta, x + \delta)\setminus C$ with $\delta\in \mathbb{Q}_{>0}$ form a countable neighborhood basis.

If $x\in A$, the sets $(x-\delta,x + \delta)\cap A$ with $\delta\in \mathbb{Q}_{>0}$ form a countable neighborhood basis.

If $x\in B$, the sets $(x-\delta,x + \delta)\cap B$ with $\delta\in \mathbb{Q}_{>0}$ form a countable neighborhood basis.
