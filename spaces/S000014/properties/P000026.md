Let $D$ be any countable subset of $(X,\tau)$. Since $D$ is countable, it misses uncountably-many elements of $X$. Choose a nonzero $x \notin D$. The set $\{x\}$ is open in $\tau$ and misses $D$, so $D$ is not dense.
