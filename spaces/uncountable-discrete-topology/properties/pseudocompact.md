Let $x_n \in X$ for $n \in \mathbb{N}$. Define $f: X \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ by $f(x_n) = n$ and $f(x) = 0$ otherwise. Then $f$ is clearly unbounded and is continuous since $X$ is discrete.
