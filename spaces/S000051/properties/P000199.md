We view {S51} as the quotient space of {S25} with the quotient map $p$, where $p^{-1}(2 i) = \{i\} \subset \mathbb R$ and $p^{-1}(2 i + 1) = (i, i + 1) \subset \mathbb R$ for all $i \in \mathbb Z$.

Define a continuous function $G \colon \mathbb R \times I \to \mathbb R$ with \[ G(x, t) = \operatorname{sgn} x \cdot \min\left\{ \left|x\right|, \frac t {1 - t} \right\}, \]
then $p \circ G$ is a continuous function from $\mathbb R \times I \to K$.

Moreover, we have \[ p(x) = p(y) \implies p \bigl( G(x, t) \bigr) = p \bigl( G(y, t) \bigr), \quad \forall x, y \in \mathbb R, \forall t \in I, \]
this induces a function $F \colon K \times I \to K$ such that $F(p(x), t) = p \bigl( G(x, t) \bigr)$, and $F$ is continuous since $p \circ G$ is continous and $p$ is a quotient map.

Actually $F$ gives a homotopy from $K$ to $\{0\}$ since $F(x, 1) = x$ and $F(x, 0) = 0$.
Therefore, $K$ is {P199}.
