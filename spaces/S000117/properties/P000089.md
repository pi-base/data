Let $L_{-1} := [0,1] \times \{0\}$. Sp for any continuous map $f:X \to X$, we find a $n \in \{-1\} \cup \mathbb{N}$, such that $f((0,0)) \in L_n$. By looking locally at path components and by intermediate value theorem, it is easy to see that $f$ then has a fixed point on $L_n$.
